Our Journey

Two paths. One living vision.

Yoga, regeneration, holistic living, community, authentic expression and the long remembering of how to live in relationship with life.

Nearly ten years of remembering.

We are Benji & Sofia: yogis, Earth lovers, regenerative learners and lifelong students of life. Nearly ten years ago, each of us began asking similar questions from different sides of the world: How can we live more truthfully? How can we become a force for good? How can our lives bring more beauty, care and positive impact into the world?

For both of us, the path began with inner awakening — yoga, breath, holistic living, spiritual inquiry, organic food, nature connection and the desire to move from separation back into relationship. Over time, that inner journey naturally became an outer one: gardens, soil, trees, food forests, community, circles, retreats and practical regeneration.

We are still learning. Still evolving. Perfectly imperfect. Sacred and silly. Raw and real. Joyful whenever possible.

Nearly ten years ago, both of our lives began changing in different parts of the world.

For Benji, the journey began after leaving Germany at twenty-five. He had been working long night shifts in industry, earning money but feeling increasingly disconnected from his health, sleep, purpose and the natural rhythm of life. Breathwork first helped him find rest again. Yoga helped him listen again. Organic living and regeneration helped him turn that awakening into practical action.

For Sofia, the journey began after leaving Colombia and the fast-paced world of PR, communications and magazines — a world of stress, image and constant performance. Her path led her towards yoga, Yin, movement, holistic living, natural healing, detoxification, authentic connection and spaces where people can soften, breathe and remember themselves.

Yoga was never only exercise.

For both of us, yoga became a way of living. A practice of returning to presence, body, breath, truth and compassion.

Over nearly a decade, yoga, meditation, breathwork, Ayurveda, conscious movement, circles, retreats and holistic living have shaped how we care for ourselves, how we hold space for others, and how we relate to the Earth.

We came to see that inner regeneration and outer regeneration are not separate. The nervous system and the ecosystem speak to one another. Healthy breath, healthy soil, healthy food, healthy relationships and healthy communities are different expressions of the same living relationship.

We did not travel the world looking for somewhere to escape to. We travelled to learn how to help places — and people — come alive again.

Benji’s path of regeneration.

Across Europe, Asia and Australia, Benji learned from regenerative farmers, biodynamic growers, syntropic pioneers, ecological educators, permaculture people, Indigenous communities, elders, yogis, retreat centres, festivals, forests and many generous teachers.

Some lessons came through books and courses. Many came through field days, long walks, pruning, composting, planting trees, observing water, listening to old gardeners and making mistakes with hands in the soil.

Over time one lesson returned again and again: regeneration is not a single technique. It is a way of seeing, relating and participating in life with humility, curiosity and love.

Sofia’s path of nourishment.

Sofia brings the softness, depth and embodiment of the inner journey. Her work is rooted in Yin Yoga, movement, presence, holistic living, nervous system care, detoxification, natural remedies and the art of creating spaces where people feel safe to be real.

Her journey reminds us that regeneration is not only about planting trees. It is also about softening the masks, releasing performance, reconnecting with the body, and remembering the quiet intelligence of the heart.

Australia changed us.

Australia also opened our eyes to the deep histories of Country and the First Peoples of the land. Learning about colonisation, dispossession and the immense suffering Indigenous peoples have endured — in Australia and across the world — changed us. We approach these histories with humility and respect, without claiming to speak for them, and with gratitude for the people and teachers who helped us understand more deeply that caring for land, culture, community and future generations are inseparable.

At the same time, Australia gifted us yoga teachers, regenerative farms, holistic communities, songs of life and love, circles, retreats, detox practices, movement, authentic connection and a deeper understanding that inner and outer regeneration belong together.

In Australia we were deeply moved by the beauty of the land, the strength of community, and the profound history of the First Peoples — the original people of the country.

Learning about colonisation, dispossession and the ongoing resilience of Indigenous communities opened our eyes to how much healing is needed around the world. We approach Indigenous wisdom with humility and respect. We do not claim to speak for any culture. We are grateful students of every teaching that helps humanity return from separation to relationship.

We also learned from yoga teachers, regenerative practitioners, holistic communities, songs, ceremonies, gardens, food forests, farmers and friends who helped us see that humanity is not broken. We carry deep capacity for love, creativity, courage and care.

From separation to interbeing.

Many of the crises of our time begin with separation: separation from nature, food, body, community, ancestry and the living Earth.

Awakening Eden is our humble attempt to participate in the return to relationship — through guides, films, yoga, circles, workshops, food forests, online education and practical land-based learning.

Casa Oasis & Zero Point.

After two deeply transformative years in Portugal, life guided us to the Serra da Estrela mountains, where we connected with Casa Oasis and the wider Zero Point vision.

Here Awakening Eden is becoming a Living Library, a community, a journal, a field guide collection and the beginning of a regenerative education and healing centre dream.
