A Living Manifesto

The Heart of Awakening Eden

A quiet invitation to remember who we are, why we are here, and the world we are choosing to grow together.

We believe a more beautiful world is not just something we wait for.

It is something we grow—from within, through love, through community, and together as one family of life.
Welcome home

If you found your way here...

Welcome. Whether you are planting your first seed, restoring a piece of land, searching for hope, dreaming of a food forest, or simply feeling called to reconnect with something deeper, you belong here.

Awakening Eden is not simply a website, a business or a collection of guides. It is a living invitation: to remember, to reconnect, to regenerate and to grow together.

Why we exist

Inspire means in-spirit.

To inspire is to lift the spirit — with the light of love, regeneration, practical solutions, beauty, courage and community.

There is no shortage of information in the world. Yet many people still feel disconnected from nature, from community, from purpose, and sometimes even from themselves.

We believe the answers we seek are not found only in more information. They are found through relationship: with the Earth, with one another, with our own hearts, and with the simple act of planting, observing, caring and sharing.

Awakening Eden exists to help nurture those relationships. Not by telling people what to think, but by walking beside them as they discover their own path and their own unique way of serving life.

Why “Awakening Eden”?

The blueprint is already within.

Every seed already carries the blueprint of the forest it can become. In much the same way, we believe every person carries an inner capacity for wisdom, compassion, creativity and stewardship.

Our role is not to give people something they do not already possess. Our role is to help create the conditions where those qualities can flourish. Like a healthy forest, life knows how to grow when given the right conditions.

Remembering

Remembering is not only recalling information. It is becoming whole again. Remembering that we belong to the Earth. Remembering that hope is practical. Remembering that healing the land and healing ourselves are not separate journeys.

Perhaps Eden is not a place we lost. Perhaps it is a way of living we can choose again — every day.

Our journey

From travelling the world to planting roots.

Nearly ten years ago, at the age of twenty-five, Benji left Germany with a backpack, an open heart and a deep curiosity about how people could live in greater harmony with the Earth.

What began as a journey across Asia and Australia slowly became a lifelong education. He immersed himself in regenerative agriculture, syntropic farming, permaculture, agroforestry, yoga, meditation and community living. He volunteered on farms, lived in retreat centres, planted trees in degraded landscapes, restored ecosystems, and learned from regenerative farmers, biodynamic growers, Indigenous communities, educators and countless people devoted to caring for life.

Meanwhile, Sofia, the same age, left Colombia to follow a similar inner calling. Her path led her to Australia, where she deepened her love for yoga, natural healing, holistic living, movement, community and practices that reconnect people with themselves and the living Earth.

Our paths crossed in the small tropical town of Mission Beach in Queensland, Australia. What began as a meeting became a shared vision.

After nearly two transformative years in Portugal, we found our way to Casa Oasis and the Zero Point vision in the Serra da Estrela mountains. It was here that Awakening Eden truly began to take root: not as a brand, but as a living expression of everything we had been learning, living and dreaming for nearly a decade.

We did not travel the world looking for somewhere to escape to. We travelled to learn how to help places — and people — come alive again.

The filter

Before publishing anything, we ask: Is it true? Is it kind? Is it practical? Is it beautiful? Is it welcoming? Does it help someone take one meaningful step?

If yes, it belongs. If not, we keep refining.

Our hope for your journey here

Whatever brings you here, you are welcome.

Perhaps you are here to learn about breathing techniques, yoga, holistic living or connecting deeply with nature.

Perhaps you are dreaming of planting a food forest, transforming your garden, restoring a farm, designing your property, building healthier soil, or taking your first small step towards regeneration.

Perhaps you are longing to sit together in sacred circle in nature — sharing authentically, vulnerably, joyfully, silently, wildly, softly, exactly as you are.

Come as you are. Take what nourishes you. Leave something beautiful behind. Then keep growing.

Your gifts are welcome. Your questions are welcome. Your curiosity, your joy, your uncertainty, your story, your pain, your purpose and your passions are welcome. We believe diversity is one of nature’s greatest strengths — and the same is true for humanity.

Every person carries something unique that the world has never seen before. Awakening Eden exists to help you remember that your life, your care and your small actions matter.

Sacred and silly

Wisdom does not always arrive wearing serious clothes.

We believe learning can be joyful, healing can be playful, and transformation does not require perfection.

Some days regeneration looks like planting a food forest. Some days it looks like laughing around a compost pile. Some days it looks like meditating at sunrise, singing to seeds, crying beneath a tree, dancing barefoot after rain, or celebrating the first tomato like excited children.

We love the deep, the prepared, the mystical, the hermetic, the spiritual and the beautifully practical. We also love the puppy-like, childlike, wonderfully silly parts of being alive.

Our way

The world does not need more people pretending to have it all figured out. It needs more people willing to be authentic, raw, present, kind, creative, playful, perfectly imperfect and learning in public.

We hope this Living Library feels less like a classroom and more like walking through a beautiful garden with good friends.
