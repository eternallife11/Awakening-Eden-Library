# Awakening Eden v23.4 — business, search and website audit

## Verdict

The v23.4 package is ready for a controlled production deployment and test. The public site is not considered updated until the production repository commit and Netlify deployment are verified.

The strongest positioning is not “general gardening.” It is:

> Regenerative property and orchard guidance that brings water, living soil, woody biomass, biodiversity, food and human capacity into one phased living system.

This creates a distinct bridge between technical land planning and Awakening Eden's deeper promise: restoring land without abandoning relationship, beauty, learning or community.

## What aligned with current demand

Current Portuguese regenerative-land providers repeatedly organise their work around diagnosis, planning, water/topography, implementation, management, measurable resilience and ongoing support. Community-focused organisations also make starting small, training people and replicable action central to their offer.

V23.4 now speaks directly to those needs without copying another business:

- property overwhelm → a clear order of action;
- dry or tired land → water, cover, biomass and living roots;
- tired orchards → phased renewal and seasonal stewardship;
- isolated design → implementation and team learning;
- one-off consultancy → an optional ongoing regeneration partnership;
- broad purpose → three simple ways to begin.

## Offer architecture

### 1. Regenerative Clarity Session

Best for remote clients or people who have many ideas and need the next 30–90 days organised.

### 2. Property Walk & 90-Day Action Map

Best first on-site offer for Central Portugal. It makes the land, constraints and opportunities visible before a larger design commitment.

### 3. Living Land Regeneration Partnership

Best for properties that need concept design, implementation support, seasonal observation and people trained to care for the system.

Specialist capabilities sit underneath these offers: orchard revival, water resilience, food forests, syntropic planting, Abundant Edge biomass systems, project support, workshops and whole-property wellbeing.

## Highest-value next upgrades after launch

1. **A real case-study page.** Add one project only when there is permission and accurate evidence: starting condition, site context, Benjy's role, actions, timeline, photographs and honest results.
2. **A Portuguese-language service page.** This will matter for local discovery and partnerships in Portugal. Translate professionally rather than automatically publishing rough copy.
3. **Confirmed fees or starting ranges.** Once Benjy decides pricing, publish a starting fee or a clear “scope and fee after first review” statement. Do not invent prices.
4. **A named service area.** Confirm the practical travel radius in Central Portugal and list towns/regions truthfully.
5. **Video and transcript.** Add the prepared short introduction video with captions and a text transcript.
6. **Proof library.** Consistently capture before, process and after images; date, place, permission, role, collaborators and lessons.
7. **Measurement that fits the work.** Where practical, record soil cover, infiltration observations, survival, diversity, shade, biomass cycles, labour and water-use changes. Do not promise outcomes that depend on climate, management or third parties.

## Market and risk guidance

- Avoid “charge the aquifer” or guaranteed water claims. Prefer “increase opportunities for infiltration,” “improve water cycling” and “develop site-specific water-resilience concepts.”
- Avoid presenting concept guidance as engineering, surveying, licensed agronomy or legal/permit advice.
- Avoid broad health or EMF promises. Keep this as practical educational guidance with qualified-professional referrals where needed.
- Do not imply photographs are paid client transformations unless that is true and documented.
- Keep the tone heart-centred, but lead each service with a real problem, deliverable and next action.
